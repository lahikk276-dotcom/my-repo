Ferrari
488
Pista
3.9L Twin-Turbo V8 · Maranello
The 488 Pista is Ferrari's track-focused evolution of the 488 GTB — Pista meaning "track" in Italian. Developed directly from the 488 GTE and 488 Challenge race programs, it carries 202 kg of carbon fiber and produces 711 hp from its 3.9-litre biturbo V8. Side air intakes feed twin turbos that spool with almost supernatural response. Every surface generates downforce. Every gram questioned. At the Fiorano circuit, the Pista set lap records that humbled supercars costing three times as much.

Jordan Collection · 02
Lamborghini
Huracán
EVO
5.2L Naturally-Aspirated V10 · Sant'Agata
The Lamborghini Huracán EVO is a masterwork of naturally-aspirated fury. Its 5.2-litre V10 screams to 8,500 RPM and produces 640 hp without a single turbocharger — raw, mechanical, visceral. LDVI (Lamborghini Dinamica Veicolo Integrata) processes 50 data points per millisecond to manage all-wheel drive, torque vectoring, and active aerodynamics in perfect harmony. The Huracán EVO is proof that the most sophisticated technology still serves the most primal emotion: joy.

Jordan Collection · 03
McLaren
720S
4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 · Woking, UK
The McLaren 720S is the most aerodynamically efficient road car ever built by the Woking firm. The MonoCell II carbon monocoque weighs just 90 kg yet forms an impenetrable safety cell. Speed-form bodywork channels air through the headlight apertures directly into the engine, eliminating conventional intakes entirely. Proactive Chassis Control II reads the road surface 1,000 times per second and pre-loads the hydraulic dampers before the wheel even contacts the bump. At 720 PS, it is mathematics made violent.

Jordan Collection · 04
Bugatti
Chiron
Super Sport
8.0L Quad-Turbo W16 · Molsheim
The Bugatti Chiron Super Sport is the pinnacle of what the internal combustion engine can achieve in road-legal form. Its 8.0-litre W16 engine — four banks of four cylinders at 90° — feeds four turbochargers sequentially to produce 1,600 hp and 1,600 Nm of torque. At full throttle, it consumes 125 litres of fuel per 100 km. The Chiron Super Sport 300+ smashed the 300 mph barrier, recording 304.773 mph on the Ehra-Lessien test track. Numbers this large stop being statistics and become mythology.

Jordan Collection · 05
Porsche
911
GT3 RS
4.0L Naturally-Aspirated Flat-6 · Stuttgart
The Porsche 911 GT3 RS is the most fanatically engineered iteration of the most fanatically engineered sports car in history. A 4.0-litre naturally-aspirated flat-six revs to 9,000 RPM and produces 525 hp — 130 hp per litre without forced induction. The swan-neck rear wing generates 409 kg of downforce at 285 km/h. Weissach Package removes every gram not contributing to lap time. The Nürburgring Nordschleife lap time of 6:49.328 made it the fastest production Porsche ever on the Ring. It is engineering as obsession.

Jordan Collection · 06
Koenigsegg
Jesko
Absolut
5.0L Twin-Turbo V8 · Ängelholm
Christian von Koenigsegg named the Jesko after his father — then gave it a top speed of 330 mph (531 km/h), the highest ever claimed for a road car. The twin-turbocharged 5.0-litre V8 produces 1,600 hp on E85 fuel. The Light Speed Transmission (LST) with its nine-clutch pack can engage any gear at any time, performing upshifts and downshifts in under 20 milliseconds. The Jesko Absolut produces zero downforce — optimized purely for straight-line terminal velocity. It may never be surpassed.

Aston Martin
Valkyrie
AMR Pro
6.5L NA V12 + Hybrid · Gaydon, UK
Co-designed with Adrian Newey — the man behind seven Formula 1 constructors' championships — the Aston Martin Valkyrie AMR Pro is the most extreme road-adjacent car ever created. Its naturally-aspirated 6.5-litre V12 spins to 11,100 RPM, its hybrid system adds another 160 hp, and the combined 1,160 hp inhabits a carbon fiber tub weighing 1,000 kg dry. The AMR Pro generates over 1,814 kg of downforce at its 225 mph limit — more than double its own weight. Not street legal. Not supposed to be.

Pagani
Huayra
Roadster BC
6.0L Twin-Turbo V12 · San Cesario
Horacio Pagani is not an engineer who makes beautiful cars — he is a sculptor who makes cars that drive. The Huayra Roadster BC is a masterpiece of carbo-titanium construction, titanium bolts, and machined aircraft aluminium. Each Huayra is personally signed by its maker. The AMG-built 6.0-litre biturbo V12 produces 800 hp, but that statistic is almost secondary to the experience of looking at it. Four active aerodynamic flaps in the corners manage airflow like a living organism. Only 40 examples were made. Each is unique.

Jordan Collection · 09
Rimac
Nevera
4×Electric Motors · Zagreb, Croatia
The Rimac Nevera holds every significant production car acceleration record in existence. 0–100 km/h in 1.85 seconds. 0–300 km/h in 9.3 seconds. A standing quarter-mile in 8.582 seconds at 249 km/h. Four electric motors — one per wheel — produce 1,914 hp and 2,360 Nm of instant torque. Mate Rimac built his first electric car from scratch in his garage at 19. The Nevera is what happened when he grew up. It doesn't just beat combustion supercars — it makes them feel ancient.
