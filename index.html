TYPE 01
Semi-Automatic
Pistol
The semi-automatic pistol is the world's most widely carried sidearm. Using the energy of each fired cartridge to cycle the action, it automatically loads the next round from a detachable box magazine housed in the grip. Compact, reliable, and fast to reload — it dominates military, law enforcement, and civilian markets globally.

TYPE 02
Revolver
Revolver
The revolver uses a rotating cylinder holding multiple individual chambers, each loaded with a cartridge. As the trigger is pulled (double-action) or hammer cocked (single-action), the cylinder rotates to align the next round with the barrel. Renowned for mechanical simplicity and reliability even after decades of neglect, revolvers remain trusted by hunters, outdoorspeople, and collectors worldwide.

TYPE 03
Long Gun
Bolt-Action
Rifle
Bolt-action rifles require the shooter to manually cycle the bolt between shots, locking the cartridge into the chamber. This deliberate process rewards accuracy — bolt guns are the benchmark for precision at distance. From big-game hunters in the Rockies to Olympic target shooters, the bolt-action's marriage of mechanical simplicity and inherent accuracy makes it an enduring classic.

TYPE 04
Smoothbore
Pump-Action
Shotgun
The pump-action shotgun cycles by sliding the fore-end rearward and forward, ejecting spent shells and chambering fresh ones. Firing shells loaded with multiple projectiles (buckshot), single slugs, or specialty rounds, the shotgun delivers devastating stopping power at close range. It dominates home defense, sport clay shooting, and upland bird hunting alike — few firearms are as versatile at short range.

TYPE 05
Automatic
Sub-Machine
Gun
The submachine gun bridges pistol and rifle — firing pistol-caliber cartridges from a compact, fully-automatic platform. Designed for close-quarters combat, SMGs provide controllable automatic fire without the size and weight of a rifle. They dominate VIP protection, police tactical units, and military special operations where hallways and vehicles dictate compact firepower. The MP5, UMP, and MAC-10 are legendary examples.

TYPE 06
Precision Long-Range
Sniper
Rifle
A precision rifle system purpose-built for extreme-range accuracy, the sniper rifle pairs a heavy, free-floated barrel with a high-magnification scope, bipod, and chassis that eliminates flex. Military configurations often include suppressors. Snipers engage targets from 500 m to over 2,000 m, making this the longest arm of infantry combat. Patience, wind reading, and trigger discipline matter as much as the hardware.

TYPE 07
Support Weapon
Light Machine
Gun
The light machine gun (LMG) provides sustained automatic fire to support infantry assault and defense. Fed by belted ammunition or large-capacity drum magazines, it can fire hundreds of rounds before overheating — unlike assault rifles. The bipod allows stable prone firing, while the heavy barrel manages heat. LMGs like the M249 SAW and RPK are the backbone of squad-level firepower, providing suppressive fire so teammates can maneuver.

TYPE 08
Selective Fire
Assault
Rifle
The assault rifle is the defining infantry weapon of the modern era — offering selective fire between semi-automatic and fully automatic modes, chambered in an intermediate cartridge that balances power with control. The AK-47, M16, and SCAR are icons of this category. Modular designs allow rapid attachment of optics, suppressors, and foregrips. Since 1944, the assault rifle has been the decisive weapon of ground combat worldwide.
